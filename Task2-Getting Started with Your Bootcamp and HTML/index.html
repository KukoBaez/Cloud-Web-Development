Cats originated from wildcats known as Felis silvestris lybica, native to the Fertile Crescent in the Near East. Their domestication began around 10,000 to 12,000 years ago during the Neolithic period, coinciding with the advent of agriculture. Early humans stored grain, which attracted rodents, and wildcats likely began living near human settlements to prey on these pests. Over time, a symbiotic relationship developed, and cats became domesticated

Some facts about cats

  • Sleepy Companions: Cats sleep an average of 12 to 16 hours a day, and some can sleep up to 20 hours, making them one of the sleepiest animals
  • Flexible and Agile: Cats have strong, flexible bodies, quick reflexes, and excellent night vision, which make them efficient hunters
  • Social but Independent: While often seen as independent, cats are actually social creatures and can form strong bonds with humans and other animals
  • Purring Mysteries: Cats purr not just when they are happy but also when they are stressed, frightened, or in pain, which is believed to have self-healing benefits
